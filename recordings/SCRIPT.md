# 🎤 Voice Fine-Tuning Script (~15 minutes)

**How to record:**
- Phone voice recorder, quiet room, natural **Indian accent** (that's the whole point — don't "neutralize" it).
- Read each line **with the feeling shown** — naturally, not robotically.
- Pause/breathe normally. If you stumble, just pause and redo the line.
- Record in a few sessions/files if easier (e.g., one file per section). Save as M4A or WAV.
- Aim for ~15 min total: ~7 min scripted below + ~8 min free talking (Part 2).

---

## PART 1 — Scripted lines (~7 min)

### 🟢 Calm / Neutral (your default meeting voice)
- "Thanks everyone for joining today. Let me quickly walk through where we are."
- "So, the main update is that the first version is ready and working as expected."
- "I'll share the document after this call so everyone has the details."
- "Let's keep this short and focus on the three things that actually matter."
- "Overall, I think we're in a good place and on track for the deadline."

### 🔴 Serious / Emphatic (slower, firm)
- "I want to be very clear about one thing before we move on."
- "If we miss this deadline, it affects the entire release. So this is the priority."
- "We cannot keep changing the plan every week. We need to commit and stick to it."
- "This is important, so please read it carefully before you reply."

### 🟡 Happy / Excited (energetic, faster)
- "This is honestly a great result — I'm really happy with how this turned out!"
- "We beat last month's numbers, and the whole team should be proud!"
- "Yes! That's exactly what I was hoping for. Let's keep this momentum going!"

### 🟣 Warm / Friendly (gentle, relaxed)
- "Hey, it's really good to see you. How have you been?"
- "No worries at all, take your time — whenever you're ready is fine."
- "Thanks so much for your help on this, I really appreciate it."

### ❓ Questions + natural pauses
- "So... where does that leave us right now?"
- "Have we checked whether the payment actually went through?"
- "Wait — did we confirm this with the client, or are we still waiting?"

### 🔢 Names + numbers + Indian context (for coverage)
- "I synced with Priya and Arjun on Tuesday the fourteenth."
- "The budget is around two lakh forty thousand rupees for this quarter."
- "Please call me at nine eight seven six five, four three two one zero."
- "The meeting is at four thirty PM on the twenty-third of June."

### 💬 Casual / conversational asides
- "Yeah, no, that makes sense — let's just park it and come back after lunch."
- "Honestly? I think we're overthinking this one a little bit."
- "Okay cool, so basically we just need to finish the testing part."

---

## PART 2 — Free talking (~8 min) — the most important part

Just **talk naturally** (no script) on these. This captures your real voice and accent best.
Speak for ~1.5–2 minutes on each:

1. **Introduce yourself** — your name, where you're from, what you're studying, what you enjoy building.
2. **Explain ClarityVoice** — what it does and why you built it (you know this well — just explain it like to a friend).
3. **Describe your day** — what you did today, in detail, casually.
4. **Tell a short story** — something funny or interesting that happened recently.
5. **Explain something you love** — a hobby, a game, a movie, a tech topic — with enthusiasm.

> Tip: free talking gives the model your *natural* rhythm and accent — this is what kills the fake American "r". Don't over-prepare; ramble a little, it's fine.

---

**Total target: ~15 minutes.** More is better and you can always add clips later and re-train.
